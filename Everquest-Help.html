1.1 Alternate Advancement

Alternate Advancement (AA) allows your character to gain abilities outside of regular level progression. You can allocate some of your earned experience into AA, which can then be spent to unlock powerful abilities (commonly referred to as AAs). You can start earning AAs as early as level 1.

Opening the AA Window:
Press [V] to open the Alternate Advancement window.
Alternatively, open your Inventory by pressing [I] and click the "Alt. Adv." button at the bottom left.

Navigating the AA Window:
The window has several tabs across the top, each containing a list of AA abilities.
Click on each tab to explore the available AAs and learn what they can do for your character.
Be sure to check the requirements listed for each ability.

Using Experience for AAs:
In the top right of the window, you'll see "Exp to AA." Here, you can choose how much of your experience will go toward AAs instead of level gain.
As you fill the AA exp bar, you'll earn AA Points, which can be spent on AA abilities.
Keep in mind that experience gained from levels 1-50 is lower per kill, based on the enemy's con, compared to levels 50+.

Learning and Using AA Abilities:
Once you have enough AA Points, click on the ability you want to learn and press the "Train" button on the right.
Some AAs are always active once learned, while others need to be activated.
To activate an AA ability, select it, then click the "Hotkey" button under the "Train" button. This will create a Hotbutton that you can place on your Hotbutton bar for easy use during gameplay.

1.2 Augmentations

Augmentations are special items that you can embed into weapons or armor to enhance their power. These items are marked with the Augmentation tag in the item information window. To view this, right-click and hold on the item. You’ll also see the slot types that the augment fits into at the bottom of the item description.

Note: Restrictions on both the augment and the item are additive when you embed the augment. For example, if the augment requires your character to be a Troll and the item requires level 30, the augmented item will require a Troll character of at least level 30.

Embedding an Augment:
Ensure that the item has the appropriate slot type for the augment (indicated in the augment's description).
To embed the augment, simply click on the augment to place it in your cursor.
With the augment on your cursor, click the highlighted augment slot on the item (the slot will turn yellow if it's compatible with the augment).

Removing Augmentations:
To remove an augment, you'll need to use an augmentation distiller, which can be purchased from the Augmentation Vendor in the Bazaar.

1.3 Auto-attack

Auto-attack is a useful feature, especially for characters who engage in melee combat. The default key to activate auto-attack is [Q]. Pressing this will cause your character to automatically attack your current target, as long as it's within melee range.

Ranged Auto-attack:
Use the command /autofire ON to enable automatic ranged attacks.
When enabled, your character will make ranged attacks as often as possible, provided you have ammunition available.
You have unlimited ammunition here on THJ, so you only need (2) arrows in the arrow slot (for safety) and (1) in the quiver, for ranged-haste.
autofire lets you attack with your bow in melee range, unlike ranged attack.
To turn off automatic ranged attacks, use the command /autofire OFF.

1.4 Auto-run

Auto-run is a feature that allows your character to run forward without needing to press any arrow keys. You can still turn left or right while using auto-run.

To toggle Auto-run:
Press the [NumLock] key on the numpad to activate.
To disable, press the [NumLock] key again or use the up/down arrow keys on your keyboard.

2.0 Acquiring, Selling, and Buying Items

The most common way to acquire items is by defeating monsters in battle and looting their corpses. Keep in mind that you can only loot an NPC corpse if the damage your group did was greater than anyone else's. It's considered good etiquette not to interfere in combat unless asked.

Looting a Corpse:
To loot a monster you’ve killed, right-click on its corpse to open its inventory.
Right-click on the items you want to keep, or click the Loot All button at the bottom to loot everything.
You can also hold the [Ctrl] key while right-clicking the corpse to loot all items at once.

Buying and Selling to a Merchant:
To interact with a merchant, right-click on them while standing nearby.
To sell an item, click on an item in your inventory, or click on an item in the merchant’s inventory to buy it.
Press the Buy or Sell button, depending on what you want to do.
For stackable items, you’ll be prompted to select the quantity you want to buy or sell. Use the arrows to adjust or type the number directly in the quantity box.

To buy/sell a full stack, hold [Shift] while left-clicking the Buy or Sell button.
To buy/sell a single item from a stack, hold [Ctrl] while left-clicking the Buy or Sell button.

Important Notes:
The price the merchant lists is per item in a stack.
The merchant will inform you if you don’t have enough money to purchase something or if they have no interest in an item you wish to sell.

Currency Conversion:
1 Platinum = 10 Gold = 100 Silver = 1000 Copper

2.1 The Bazaar

The Bazaar is a central hub in The Heroes' Journey (THJ). It houses important NPCs, vendors, specialized merchants, service NPCs, and is where players can set up automated traders to sell their goods to other players. Every player is autogranted the travel skill Bazaar and Back via their Alternate Advancement window on the General Tab. This skill allows players to travel to the Bazaar from anywhere in the world and return to their original location by clicking the skill again.

Key Vendors and NPCs:
A Faded Memory - This quest NPC, appearing as a ghostly figure, is located next to the western-most bank and marks the start of a player's journey in THJ.
Tearel - Located in the Bazaar, Tearel offers teleportation to various destinations across the THJ world. To unlock these destinations, visit stone circles in key areas and attune your Magic Map. Once attuned, use the floating Magic Map on Tearel's desk to teleport instantly.
Donation Merchant - This vendor sells donator items like large bags for Echo of Memory (EOM), a currency earned through server donations.
Donation Server Buff Merchant - Exchange EOM for server-wide buffs that benefit all players on the server.
Polymorphist - Offers race and gender changes. Name and pet name changes will be available in the future.
Mischievous Halfling - Runs the casino in the Bazaar. For 5000 platinum pieces (pp) or 2 EOM, you can try your luck for a chance to win useful items.
Purveyor of Glamour - For 5000pp and an item, she will transform it into a Glamour-Stone, which can be used as a weapon ornament to change the look of your weapon. You can also trade EOM for a random Glamour-Stone, offering a chance at something unique.
Guildmasters (GM) - Essential NPCs for selecting your initial classes. Hail the GM and follow the text prompts to add new classes.

Browsing and Buying from Player Traders:
To find items from player traders, type /bazaar to open the Bazaar Search Window.
In the search window, you can enter the name or partial name of an item you're looking for. Use the filters below to narrow your search.
Once you find an item you're interested in, either visit the trader in person at a covered stall or click the Buy button to purchase it for an additional fee. The item will be delivered instantly to the Parcel vendor near the bankers.
To buy from a trader in person, right-click the merchant to open their shop window and purchase items directly.

Becoming a Merchant:
To become a merchant, purchase a Trader’s Satchel from the Bag Merchant in the Bazaar.
Place the items you wish to sell into your satchel, then head to a covered bazaar platform. Type /trader to open the Bazaar Vendor window.
Use the Set Price button to set prices for your items (for stacked items, you're setting the price for one item in the stack).
Once you're ready, click Begin Trader to start selling your goods as a merchant.

3.0 Camping and Logging Out

When you are not actively playing, your character does not require food, drink, or rest. Any conditions your character was in (such as being tired or hungry) will persist when you log back in. Additionally, your character will remain in the same location where you left the game.

Logging Out:
Before exiting the game, move your character to a safe area whenever possible.
Type /camp to begin the camping process. It will take about 30 seconds for your character to leave the game.
After camping, you'll be taken to the character select screen, where you can either choose to play a different character or leave the game.

Returning to the Game:
When you log back in, your character will be where you left them. Be sure to choose a safe location to camp, as enemies may be nearby when you log back in.

Additional /camp Commands:
/camp server - Camps you to the server select screen.
/camp desktop - Exits the game completely.
Typing /q exits the game immediately!

Compass Icon: The Compass icon at the bottom right opens the mapping toolbar, where you can create your own maps.

3.2 Chat Channels

EverQuest supports up to ten player-created chat channels, in addition to the pre-existing channels. These channels are different from the in-game communication channels mentioned in section 2.2. Players can create their own channels, and a few permanent channels are provided by the game team. To communicate in these channels, you must join them and use the correct commands to send messages.

Chat Channel Commands:
/join channel name:channel password - Joins a chat channel.
/chat set channel1, channel2...channel10 - Join up to ten chat channels at once. This will overwrite any existing channels.
/#[text] - Sends a message to a specific chat channel (replace # with a channel number 1–10).
/autojoin channel1:password, channel2...channel10 - Automatically joins the specified channels when you log in.
/leave channel # - Leaves a particular chat channel.
/leaveall - Leaves all chat channels you are currently in.
/list - Lists all the chat channels you are currently in.

Note: You can replace /chat and / with a semicolon (;) in most cases.

You can chat to OOC by typing /ooc, this is a global channel that is integrated with discord's own ⁠⌨｜ooc channel.

You cannot leave the OOC channel at the moment, but you can send it to its own window and minimize it.

3.3 Chat Specific Commands

Here are some commands to help you manage your chat experience:

/channel [channel name] - Sets the default chat channel for the chat window you type it in. For example, typing /channel ooc sets the default channel to Out of Character (OOC). From then on, every message you type in that window will be sent as a shout. To view the list of available channels, type /channel with no arguments.

/tell [Charactername] - Sends a private tell to the specified character.

/msg [Charactername] - Same as the /tell command; sends a private message to the specified character.

/reply - Sends a reply to the last person who sent you a tell. Press [R] to automatically type their character’s name. After pressing [R], you can press [TAB] to cycle through the names of the last 10 people who sent you a tell.

/yell - Sends a yell for help to those within a short radius of you. Typically used when you are in need of assistance.

3.4 Combat Specific Commands

These commands are essential for managing combat and related actions:

/assist [TARGET] - If your target is within 200 feet, this command automatically targets whoever that character is targeting. Great for group combat situations where everyone focuses on the same target.

/assist ON/OFF - By default, when you use /assist, auto-attack turns on. This command toggles auto-attack on or off based on your preference.

/attack - Turns attack mode on or off.

/autosplit ON/OFF - Automatically splits coins you loot with party members when turned on.

/book - Opens or closes your spellbook.
/book [1-50] - Opens your spellbook to the specified page (1–50).

/cast [1-12] - Casts the spell in the specified memorization slot (1–12).

/doability [1-6] - Performs the associated ability from the "Abilities" page in the main menu.
/doability [7-10] - Performs the associated combat skill.

/loot - Allows you to loot a corpse from a short distance (corpse must be targeted).

/stopsong - A Bard-specific command that stops the bard’s song. This is useful in a hotkey setup.

/stopcast - Instantly stops casting a spell. This is useful in a hotkey but doesn’t work while mounted.

3.5 Communication

The chat window is your main tool for communication with other players. Here are some key chat channels and their uses:

/shout (message) - Zonewide channel. Use sparingly to get the attention of everyone in the zone.

/auction (message) - Zonewide channel. Used for selling goods to other players.

/ooc (message) - Gamewide channel. Integrated with the ⁠⌨｜ooc chat on discord.

/say (message) - Local channel. Used for talking to those in your immediate area.

/gsay (message) - Groupwide channel. For talking to your group members, regardless of zone. Abbreviated as /g.

/guild (message) - Guildwide channel. Used to communicate with everyone in your guild, regardless of zone. Abbreviated as /gu.

Notes:
The default chat setting is /say.
Please refrain from using inappropriate language in any chat channels.

3.6 The Consider (/Con) System

The /Con system helps you assess the difficulty of an opponent. To use it, right-click on your target or left-click on them and type /con. You'll then see one of the following colored messages in your chat window, indicating the relative difficulty of the target:

  • Gray: This target is significantly below your skill level and offers no experience.
  • Green: This target is below your skill level and offers reduced experience.
  • Blue: This target is somewhat below your skill level.
  • Dark Blue: This target is an adequate challenge and offers an experience bonus.
  • White: This target is an equivalent challenge and offers an experience bonus.
  • Yellow: This target is more skilled than you and offers an experience bonus.
  • Red: This target is far more skilled than you and offers an experience bonus.

Note: These challenge levels are designed assuming you'll be in a group at higher levels. As your character gains levels, NPCs that /con the same will become greater challenges.

The Dye System has been mostly replaced with the new Armor Trangsmog system, through the purveyor of glamor in the bazaar, but some facets still remain.

To apply dye to your armor, follow these steps:

  1. Open the Armor Dye window: Press [ I ] to open your inventory, then click on the Dye Button.
  2. Select the Armor Location: Find the row corresponding to the armor location you want to dye. Then click on the box under the Armor Dye column.
  3. Choose a Color: A Choose a Color window will appear. You can:
    • Pick from pre-created colors.
    • Create your own custom color using RGB values.

Reagents: Every color change requires a reagent. The total number of reagents needed for all your changes will display at the bottom of the Armor Dye window. You will see the changes on your character as you apply them.

4.1 Dynamic Zone and Task Commands

Dynamic Zones (also known as Expeditions) are group instances with specific mechanics and commands. Most of the commands below require you to be the leader of your dynamic zone.

Dynamic Zone (Expedition) Commands:
/dzhelp - Displays general help regarding dynamic zones.
/dzaddplayer [player name] - Adds the targeted or specified player to your dynamic zone. Works across zone lines.
/dzremoveplayer [player name] - Removes the targeted or specified player from your dynamic zone. Works across zone lines.
/dzswapplayer CHARACTERNAMEtoREMOVE CHARACTERNAMEtoADD - Replaces one player in the DZ with another. The second player must accept the invitation and be eligible to join.
/dzmakeleader [character name] - Transfers dynamic zone leadership to the specified character.
/dzlisttimers - Displays all your current dynamic zone lockout timers.
/dzplayerlist - Lists the leader and all members of the current dynamic zone.
/compareexped - Compares the list of players in your raid with those in your dynamic zone, showing mismatches.
/kickplayers exp - Removes all players, including yourself, from the current dynamic zone.

Task Commands:
These commands manage Shared Tasks. Most are also accessible via the Shared Task tab in your Quest Journal ([Alt + Q]).

/taskadd [character name] - Adds the targeted or specified player to your shared task.
/taskremove [character name] - Removes the targeted or specified player from your shared task.
/taskplayerlist - Lists all players currently in your shared task.
/taskmakeleader [character name] - Transfers task leadership to the targeted or specified player (must already be in the task).
/tasktimers - Displays all your task-related lockout timers.
/taskquit - Removes yourself from your current shared task.
/comparetask - Compares the characters in your shared task with those in your raid, showing mismatches.
/kickplayers task - Removes all players, including yourself, from the current shared task.

Notes:
Ensure you have leadership privileges to use most of these commands.
These commands can streamline group and raid organization within dynamic zones and shared tasks.

5.2 Evolving Items

Evolving Items are a special category of items that improve and change over time based on your use. These items grow stronger as you kill monsters and gain experience, much like your character does!

How to Identify Evolving Items:
To check if an item is evolving, right-click and hold on the item in your inventory.
Look for the Evolving status line, which will display the item’s current level and experience progress towards the next level.

How Evolving Items Gain Experience:
Evolving Items gain experience as you kill monsters.
The experience gained is bonus experience, meaning there is no loss of experience for your character.
In order for the item to gain experience, it must be equipped in your Power Slot (the designated slot for such items).
Items that are not bags or already legendary can evolve.

Evolution Process:
Evolving Items level up in stages, just like a player:
Base item to Enchanted
Enchanted to Legendary

Every time the item gains experience and reaches the next level, you will receive a notification.

Item Effects and Requirements:
Some Evolving Items have effects that require a certain level to be used.
If you are not high enough level to use a new effect from the item, you will get a message indicating this.

Transferring Experience Between Evolving Items:
Some Evolving Items can transfer their experience to one another.
To do this, use the Consume Item ability (AA). This allows you to transfer experience from one item to another of the same type.
For example, Painbringer can be consumed to add experience to another Painbringer item. To do this:
Hold the item in your cursor.
Click the Consume Item AA from your shortcut bar.

This system adds a new layer of progression to your items, encouraging you to actively use and evolve them alongside your character’s growth!

5.3 Experience

In EverQuest, gaining experience is a crucial part of your progression. Here's a breakdown of how it works:

Gaining Experience:
Combat: The primary way to gain experience is by defeating monsters in the world of Norrath.
Quests: Completing quests also rewards you with experience points.

Viewing Your Experience:
To view your current experience and level progress, open your inventory by pressing the [I] key.
Your experience information is located beneath the "NEXT LEVEL" label on the left side of the inventory window.

Understanding the Experience Bar:
The orange bar represents your progress toward the next level. As you gain experience, this bar fills up.
The blue bar in the center shows approximately 20% of your total experience required for the next level.

Leveling Up:
Once the orange bar reaches its end, your character will level up and become more powerful.
Hit Points (HP) increase, allowing you to survive longer in combat.
Mana (for casters) increases, letting you cast more spells.
Endurance improves, enabling you to perform more physical actions like running and using abilities.
Many skills can be raised as you level up, allowing you to improve your abilities.

Benefits of Leveling:
Power increase: With every level gained, you become stronger overall, gaining more survivability, abilities, and potential to take on tougher challenges in the game.

5.4 Extended Target Window

The Extended Target Window in EverQuest expands your ability to track multiple targets at once, making it a powerful tool for both group and raid situations.

How to Access:
Click the EQ button on your UI.
Navigate to the 'Actions' tooltip.
From there, you'll find the Extended Target Window.

By default, the slots in the Extended Target Window are automatically filled with NPCs (or enemies) that are attacking you or your group/raid members. However, you can customize these slots to show whatever targets you wish.

Targeting in the Extended Target Window:
Primary Target: You can click on any of the targets in the Extended Target Window to make it your primary target, as long as it is within targeting range.
Customization: Right-click on any slot in the window to access various customization options.

Useful Features and Slots:
Puller's Target: This shows the target of your group's designated puller. It’s useful for pre-targeting the incoming NPCs before they get close.
Group Assist's Target: This slot shows the target of the group member who is designated as Group Assist.
Target's Target: This slot shows the target of your current target. If your target is an NPC, it will show who the NPC is targeting.

Why Use the Extended Target Window:
It allows you to track multiple targets simultaneously.
Helps with coordinated efforts.
Can help you stay aware of what enemies are targeting.

6.2 Food and Drink

Food and drink play a vital role in maintaining your character's well-being on The Heroes' Journey. If you fail to keep a moderate supply of food and water, your character will experience negative effects such as:

  • Hungry: "You are hungry."
  • Thirsty: "You are thirsty."

Without food and drink, your character will no longer regenerate health, mana, or stamina, which can severely hinder your ability to continue adventuring.

How It Works:
Automatic Consumption: Your character will eat and drink automatically as long as they have food and drink in their inventory. The character will automatically consume the first food and drink items in your inventory, from left to right and top to bottom.
Force Consumption: To force your character to eat or drink a specific item, right-click on the food or drink in your inventory.

Benefits:
Stat-Increasing Foods: Some food and drink will temporarily increase stats like health or stamina. These effects will only apply if the stat-boosting food or drink is the first item in your inventory.

Special Notes:
Alcohol: Alcohol does not count as a sustaining drink and will not be consumed automatically to quench thirst. It must be consumed manually by right-clicking on it.

7.0 Group and Raid Commands

Managing groups and raids is an essential part of teamwork in The Heroes' Journey. Below are the commands to help you organize and communicate within these groups:

Group Commands:
/invite CHARACTERNAME: Invites the specified player or your current target to join your group. This can only be executed by the group leader.
/disband CHARACTERNAME: Allows a character to leave the group. The group leader can also disband a specific member if targeted.
/gsay (or /g): Sends a message to all members of your group.

Raid Commands:
/rsay (or /rs): Sends a message to everyone in your raid.
/raidinvite CHARACTERNAME: Invites the specified player or your current target to join your raid. If the player is already in a group, their whole group will be added to the raid.
/raidaccept: Accepts an invitation to join a raid.
/raiddecline: Declines an invitation to join a raid.
/raiddisband CHARACTERNAME: Allows a character to leave a raid if they are targeted. The raid leader can also remove a specified member from the raid.

Loot Management (Raid Leader Commands):
/setloottype #: This sets the loot rights for the raid. The options are:
1: Raid Leader Only
2: Raid Leader and Group Leaders Only
3: Raid Leader and Selected Raid Members Only
4: Entire Raid (Free For All)

/addraidlooter CHARACTERNAME: Specifies a player who is allowed to loot items, but this will only work if the loot type is set to option 3.
/removeraidlooter CHARACTERNAME: Removes a player's loot rights. This will only work if the loot type is set to option 3.

These commands allow you to effectively manage and organize your group or raid, whether you're coordinating for quests, battles, or loot distribution.

7.1 Grouping

Grouping is a fantastic way to experience social interaction and collaboration in The Heroes' Journey. It allows you to team up with others for quests, dungeons, and other activities, enhancing both your gameplay and social experience. Here's how you can manage and enjoy grouping:

Looking for Group (LFG):
/lfg on: Toggles a flag next to your name, signaling to others that you're looking for a group. This flag will show up in the /who command and above your character's nameplate. You can also check for others with the LFG flag and invite them to your group.

Inviting Players to Group:
[CTRL + I]: While targeting a player, this invites them to join your group. It's a quick way to get new members into your team.

Leaving a Group:
[CTRL + D]: If you're in a group and wish to leave, press F1 to target yourself, then press [CTRL + D] to disband. As a group leader, [CTRL + D] will disband the targeted member from your group.

Group Communication:
/gsay TEXT: Use this command to send a message exclusively to your group. This ensures that only group members can see your message, making it ideal for group-specific plans, strategies, or just casual conversation.

Teleporting Group Members:
As a group leader, you may also have the ability to teleport group members using the Bazaar and Back AA abilities, which can be helpful for quickly moving around zones and dungeons.

By grouping, you not only get stronger through collaboration but also have the opportunity to make new friends and connections.

7.2 Guild Commands

Guilds are a fundamental part of The Heroes' Journey, providing a space for players to form lasting teams, share resources, and communicate with like-minded adventurers. Here are some essential guild commands:

Guild Communication:
/guildsay TEXT or /gu TEXT: Sends a message to all online members of your guild. This is a great way to communicate with your entire guild at once.

Guild Management:
/guildinvite TARGET: Invite players to join the guild.
/guildremove CHARACTERNAME: Removes a player from the guild.
/guilddelete: Guild leaders can disband their guild entirely.

Guild Information:
/guildstatus TARGET: Returns the guild status of the named character or the target, including their guild rank (same zone only).

Transferring Guild Leadership:
/guildleader TARGET: Transfers the guild leadership to the specified target character (usually an officer).

Guild Message of the Day (MOTD):
/guildmotd TEXT: Officers can set the guild's Message of the Day (MOTD), shown to all members on login (200-character limit).

7.4 Guild Management

Guild Management allows you to handle various administrative tasks within your guild. Here’s how you can manage your guild effectively:

Opening Guild Management:
Press [ ALT + G ] or click the Angle Brackets icon in the Window Selector to open the Guild Management window.

Sorting the Guild Member List:
Click the header of any column (e.g., name, rank, status) to sort.

Online/Offline Status:
White = online
Gray = offline

Notes:
Public Notes can be created by leaders/officers, visible to all.
Private Notes can be created by any member, visible only to that member.

Message of the Day (MOTD):
Leaders/officers can set the MOTD. It resets if the server goes down.

Promotions, Demotions, and Leadership:
Leaders can promote/demote or transfer leadership.
Click Remove to kick a member from the guild.

7.5 Guilds

A guild is an in-game association of player characters that allows you to form stronger ties with other players. Joining a guild can make grouping and raiding more efficient and rewarding, especially when you find a group with similar goals.

Guild Window
Press [ ALT + G ]. If you are not a member of a guild, the window will prompt you to create one.

Creating a Guild
If you're not in a guild, you'll be asked if you want to create one.
Enter a guild name and click Create.
Once created, you can invite friends, chat with other guild members (/guildsay TEXT), and promote members to officer status.

If you’re considering joining or creating a guild, make sure the guild’s values and goals align with yours.

8.0 Hotbuttons

Hotbuttons provide quick access to various actions, items, and spells in EverQuest, allowing for efficient gameplay and ease of use. Here’s how you can utilize and customize your Hotbuttons:

Hotbutton Windows:
Two horizontal windows at bottom-left, each with ten buttons by default.
Each window has 10 pages of 10 buttons = 100 Hotbuttons per window.

Creating a Hotbutton (Drag and Drop):
Left-click and hold an item/spell/skill, then drag it to a hotbutton slot. Release to assign.

Creating a Hotbutton (Command Method):
/hotbutton BUTTONNAME
Then right-click the created Hotbutton to edit commands (spell, item, emotes, etc.).

Editing Hotbuttons:
Right-click to open an edit window for customizing the Hotbutton’s actions.

9.0 Memorizing Spells

Now that you have scribed a spell into your book, you can memorize it and therefore be able to cast it.

Persistent Buffs:
On The Heroes' Journey, buffs you cast will never drop off your character as long as the spell you used to cast it is memorized. You have 460 spell slots, and you can right-click the spellbook at the bottom of your spellgems to save spell sets for quick loading between your extensive collection of spells.

Accessing the Spell Gems Window:
Locate Your Spell Gems Window at the bottom right of your screen.
Toggle on/off with [ ALT + S ] if needed.

Ways to Memorize a Spell:
1) Using the Spell Book
2) Using the Spell Gems Window Menu

Hold [ALT] over a gem to see the name/duration. Right-click & hold to see more info about the spell.

9.1 Quick Cash

When you start, your character will have enough food and drink to last for a while, but eventually you are going to need to purchase more. Perhaps you'll want better armor or spells. For this, you will need money.

The first quest you receive in The Heroes' Journey, "The journey of a thousand miles...", will give you enough money to get started if you follow the steps in your quest journal.

Best Way to Earn Money:
Defeat enemies outside the city for coins. Loot their corpses and sell items to merchants.

NOTE: 1 Platinum = 10 gold = 100 silver = 1000 copper

9.2 Reagents

Some spells require that you have a component to cast. These components are used to focus the power of the spell from its ethereal form to a physical one and are typically consumed during the casting of the spell. It is often good to keep a supply of reagents handy, especially when grouping with other characters.

Where to Find Components:
You can find all the components you need in the Bazaar at the components vendor!

9.3 Scribing Spells

In order to cast a spell, your character will have to do two things:
1) Scribe the spell into your spell book.
2) Memorize the spells from your book that you would like to be able to cast.

Requirements to Scribe a Spell:
Own the scroll, be high enough level, not already in your spellbook.

Steps to Scribe a Spell:
Open your spellbook ([CTRL + B]).
Drag the scroll over an empty slot, left-click it to start scribing. This may take a while.

Moving a Memorized Spell:
Right-click the current location, select the new slot, etc.

9.4 Skills

Your character possesses a few basic skills at the start of the game. For example: Beg, Fish, Bind Wounds, Sense Heading

Some skills, such as Elvish or Pick Lock, are only known to characters of a specific race or class. Other skills that you don't initially know can be learned as you gain levels and experience.

Checking Your Skills:
On The Heroes' Journey, you can use #myskills to check your skills in an extended window.

Assigning Skills to Hotkeys:
Press [ I ] to open inventory, then click Skills. Click a skill, click Make Hotkey, place on your hotkey bar.

Additional AA Skills:
Bazaar Portal, Bazaar and Back, Consume Item. Use #myskills for skill values.

9.5 Songs Window

Look on your display for a window with a translucent background titled Songs. This is where Bard songs affecting your character are displayed.

Bard Songs vs. Spells:
Bard songs have short-duration pulsing effects, displayed in the Songs window, while spells generally have longer durations (some short spells also appear here).

Interacting with Songs:
Right-click & hold to learn more. Left-click to remove it if beneficial. All spells in the Songs window are removed when you zone.

Note: Not all bard songs are filtered to this window; a few occupy an Effects slot.

13.1 Mail and Parcels

You can send messages and even items to other players in the game!

Open your mail window to compose messages, which works similarly to email.

Sending a Parcel
Visit a Parcel Merchant in major cities or the Bazaar. Right-click them, go to Parcels tab, enter the recipient's name, etc. Must be tradable items only.

13.2 Magical Weapons

Some monsters require a magical weapon to be harmed. If you don't have a magical weapon equipped, these targets will be invulnerable to your attacks.

Tip: It's wise to get a magical weapon early. Monks might need magical boots if they only do 1 damage with kicks, etc.

13.3 The Marketplace

The Marketplace as it exists in EverQuest does not exist here on The Heroes' Journey. Instead, you are given Echoes of Memory for donations.

Plenty of places to spend EoM, such as Donation Merchant, Buffs NPC, Polymorphist, etc.

13.4 Movement

The keyboard is your main control input for movement in EverQuest, while the mouse controls the cursor and your point of view. By default, movement keys are Arrow Keys or [W], [A], [S], [D].

Other Default Movement Keys:
Auto-run = [NumLock]
Duck = [X]
Jump = [Spacebar]
Look Up = [PGUP] or [NumPad 9]
Look Down = [PGDOWN] or [NumPad 3]

Auto-run allows your character to run forward without holding movement keys. Press [NumLock] to toggle on/off.

13.5 Mouse-look

Mouse-look is a feature that lets you use your mouse to look around. Right-click-and-drag to look around.

Running with Mouse-look:
Hold left mouse while in mouse-look to move forward.

Known Issue (Emulators):
May vary in speed. Set your FPS limit to 60 in the display options.

16.2 Player Character Death

When your character dies, a Respawn Options window will pop up. You typically respawn at your Bind Location, with items intact.

Experience Loss
Characters over level 5 lose a small amount of XP on death.

Corpses
Above level 5, your corpse remains. Others may resurrect you to regain XP.

Corpse Decay
Corpses last ~7 days before decaying.

Dragging Your Corpse
Use /corpse or /consent Playername for others to drag.

Hiding Corpses
/hidecorpse commands to filter which corpses are visible.

16.3 Player Window

The Player Window displays your character’s name, plus three bars (Health, Mana, Endurance).

Health Bar (Red) - If it hits zero, you die.

Mana Bar (Blue) - For casting spells.

Endurance (Yellow) - For physical actions.

Combat Indicator
Shows OOC if you’re out of combat, swords if in combat, poison drop if under detrimental effects, hourglass if recently in combat.

Unread In-Game Mail
An envelope icon appears if you have unread mail. [CTRL + E] to read.

16.4 Power Sources

In The Heroes' Journey, your Power Source slot is used to grant experience to evolving items. Items progress from Normal ➡️ Enchanted ➡️ Legendary.

The first quest "The journey of a thousand miles..." explains how this process works with your Simple Ring.

Pro Tip: Always keep something in your power slot to level it up.

23.0 The /who Command

The /who command is a very useful tool that allows you to see who is online within specific search parameters.

Basic Usage:
Type /who in the chat bar and hit enter to see everyone currently in your zone.

Worldwide Search:
Use /who all to search beyond your current zone.

Examples:
/who all necromancer — Lists all necromancers in the world.
/who all 5 — Lists all level 5 characters.
/who all 5 10 — Lists all characters between levels 5 and 10.
/who all cleric 3 — Lists all level 3 clerics.
/who all "Guild Name" — Lists guild members for "Guild Name" (quotes required).
/who all Maddoc — Returns any character whose name begins with "Maddoc."

If the list is too large, it may cut short. Characters who are anonymous or role-playing will not appear in your search unless they are in your zone.
